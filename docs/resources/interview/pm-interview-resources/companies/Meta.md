# Meta — PM Interview Profile

## Company Overview
- **Mission:** Give people the power to build community and bring the world closer together
- **Products:** Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, Messenger, Threads, Meta Quest (VR), Ray-Ban smart glasses
- **Culture:** Move fast, data-driven, high accountability. Year of Efficiency (2023) shifted culture toward leaner teams and higher output expectations.
- **Size:** ~70,000 employees (post-layoffs)
- **Business model:** Advertising (~98% of revenue), with growing hardware/VR bets

---

## What Meta Looks For in PMs

Meta's PM framework: **Understand → Identify → Execute**
- **Understand** the people problem you're solving
- **Identify** the best way to start solving it
- **Execute** that solution flawlessly

**Key traits:**
- **Data fluency** — Meta has one of the most sophisticated experimentation platforms in tech. PMs must be deeply comfortable with metrics, A/B testing, and RCA.
- **Product strategy** — Strong market thinking, ability to evaluate competitive moves
- **Prioritization under chaos** — Meta's roadmaps shift fast. Ability to say "no" is critical.
- **Cross-functional collaboration** — PMs work across eng, data science, design, policy, legal
- **Communication at all levels** — From IC to VP; must adapt style
- **Social product instincts** — Understand social graphs, network effects, community dynamics
- **Technical background NOT required** — Meta is more product-strategy oriented than technical PM focused

---

## Interview Process (PM Roles)

1. **Resume screen**
2. **Recruiter screen** (~30 min) — Background, why Meta, behavioral warm-up
3. **PM phone screen** (~45 min) — Mix of product sense + execution; conversational
4. **On-site loop** (3-4 rounds, 45 min each):
   - **Product Sense** — Design/improve a product; business cases, user pain points, tradeoffs
   - **Product Execution** — Metrics-heavy; prioritization, RCA, decision-making
   - **Leadership & Drive** — Behavioral; conflict, influence, ownership
   - **Hiring Manager chat** — Culture fit, team match

**Key insight from candidates:** "Thinking out loud is huge. They care more about your reasoning than the right answer."

---

## Meta's Key Interview Types

### Product Sense (Design)
- They push you to think **long and deep** on user pain points and tradeoffs
- Always tie solutions back to Meta's family of apps (Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp) rather than standalone products
- Business context matters — how does this affect Meta's ad business?

### Product Execution (Analytics)
- Very metrics heavy — know your DAU/MAU, engagement, retention, revenue metrics
- RCA format is common: "A key metric for Product A increased but the same metric for Product B decreased. What do you do?"
- Prioritization frameworks expected

### Leadership & Drive (Behavioral)
- Standard STAR format
- Focus: influence without authority, resolving conflict, making decisions with incomplete data

## Tips Specific to Meta

- **Know Meta's products deeply** — They ask a lot of questions about existing products; shallow knowledge will hurt you
- **Always ground in data** — Every answer needs metrics. "I would look at the data" is not enough — specify which data.
- **Think in network effects** — Meta's moat is its social graph. Factor this into product thinking.
- **Address the ad business** — Even consumer-facing features have ad revenue implications at Meta
- **Speed matters** — Meta moves fast; show you can ship and iterate, not just plan
- **Competitors to know:** TikTok, Snapchat, YouTube, X (Twitter), Apple iMessage
