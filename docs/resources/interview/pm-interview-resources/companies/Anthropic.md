# Anthropic — PM Interview Profile

## Company Overview
- **Mission:** The responsible development and maintenance of advanced AI for the long-term benefit of humanity
- **Known for:** Claude (AI assistant), Constitutional AI, safety research, interpretability work
- **Culture:** High-trust, low-ego, mission-first. Less hierarchy than traditional tech. Ownership mentality — "if something urgently needs to be done, you're probably the right person to do it."
- **Size/Stage:** ~1,000 employees (2025), growing fast. Still operates with startup agility.
- **B Corp** — legally bound to consider societal impact alongside profit

---

## What Anthropic Looks For in PMs

- **Mission alignment** — Genuine care about AI safety, not just interest in AI. They can tell the difference.
- **Technical depth** — You don't need to be an engineer, but you must understand LLMs, safety tradeoffs, and the research-to-product pipeline
- **Written reasoning** — Anthropic is a writing-heavy culture. PMs write detailed PRDs, post-mortems, and safety reviews. Clarity of thought on paper matters enormously.
- **Comfort with ambiguity** — Products are novel, research timelines are uncertain, user needs are emerging
- **Low ego / high ownership** — You'll work in small teams with high autonomy. No room for politics or credit-seeking.
- **Safety-product balance** — Can you reason about capability vs. safety tradeoffs without being dogmatic in either direction?
- **Startup experience valued** — Ability to sell technical products to technical stakeholders is a strong signal

---

## Interview Process (PM Roles)

1. **Recruiter screen** (~30 min) — Background, why Anthropic, salary (don't reveal early)
2. **Hiring manager interview** — How you think through problems, mission fit
3. **Take-home assignment** — Written memo or product exercise (quality of reasoning > polish)
4. **On-site loop** — Mix of product, behavioral, and cross-functional rounds
   - Product/Strategy round
   - Analytical/Metrics round
   - Cross-functional collaboration round (working with eng/research)
   - Behavioral/leadership round

**Note:** AI use is strictly prohibited during live interviews. Process takes ~3-4 weeks. They don't give exploding offers.

---

## How to Stand Out

- Tie every answer back to the safety mission — not performatively, but genuinely
- Summarize Anthropic research posts before interviews (interpretability, Constitutional AI, RSP)
- Demonstrate you've used Claude extensively and can speak to its UX strengths and gaps
- Show written clarity — answers should be structured like a concise memo
- Bring examples of navigating ambiguity + technical complexity in past roles

---

## Key Topics to Know

- **Constitutional AI** — Anthropic's method for training helpful, harmless, honest models
- **RLHF / RLAIF** — How models are aligned with human feedback
- **Hallucination** — When and why models generate false information; mitigation strategies
- **Agentic AI** — Multi-step autonomous AI actions; unique safety/UX challenges
- **Responsible Scaling Policy (RSP)** — Anthropic's framework for when/how to deploy more capable models
- **Interpretability** — Research into understanding what's happening inside neural networks
- **Claude product line** — Claude.ai consumer product, API, Claude for Enterprise, Claude Code

## Confirmed Interview Questions (Real, Sourced)

### AI / Technical
- How do you approach GenAI safety in consumer products?
- How would you handle hallucinations in a generative AI model deployed to users?
- What Gen AI skills do you think will be critical in the next year?
- What's your approach to prompt engineering?
- How would you explain a technical concept to a non-technical person?
- Define hallucinations in LLMs.
- What are the ethical risks of deploying agentic AI systems in high-stakes environments?
- What are the risks of assuming LLMs think or feel like humans?
- How would you adapt a generative AI model to perform well in a specific domain?

### Product / Strategy
- Brainstorm a product idea with the help of AI.
- How would you launch a new product to our existing user base?
- How would you grow Anthropic's revenue without compromising the safety mission?
- How would you measure success for Claude?
- What's the biggest threat to Anthropic's market position?

### Behavioral
- Why do you want to work at Anthropic?
- Tell me about a time you had a conflict with someone. How did you resolve it and what did you learn?
- Tell me about a time you made a bold and difficult decision.
- How do you prioritize tasks?
- Tell me about a time when you received negative feedback and how you handled it.
- Tell me about a time when you faced a conflict while on a team.
- Tell me about a time when you had to deliver negative feedback.
- How do you deal with ambiguous situations?
- What are your long-term goals, outside of work?

## Tips Specific to Anthropic

- Don't just say you care about AI safety — show you understand the specific tensions (capability vs. safety, speed vs. caution, openness vs. risk)
- Know the difference between Claude.ai (consumer), Claude API (developer), and Claude for Enterprise
- Be ready to discuss: "How would you balance shipping fast with maintaining safety standards?"
- Anthropic values written clarity — if given a take-home, treat it like a crisp 1-page memo
