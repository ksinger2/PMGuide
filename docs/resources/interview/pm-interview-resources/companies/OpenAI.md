# OpenAI — PM Interview Profile

## Company Overview
- **Mission:** Ensure that artificial general intelligence benefits all of humanity
- **Products:** ChatGPT (consumer + enterprise), API (GPT-4, DALL-E, Whisper, Sora), Operator, GPT Store, OpenAI for Business
- **Culture:** Fast-moving, research-forward, high-stakes. Tension between safety and capability is real and ongoing. Post-2023 leadership crisis, culture has evolved but remains ambitious and intense.
- **Size:** ~3,000 employees (2025), growing fast
- **Business model:** API revenue, ChatGPT Plus/Pro/Team/Enterprise subscriptions, partnership with Microsoft

---

## What OpenAI Looks For in PMs

- **Deep AI product intuition** — You must understand LLMs at a meaningful depth: capabilities, limitations, failure modes, and what makes AI products uniquely hard to design
- **Safety + capability balance** — Can you reason about when to ship vs. when to hold back? Do you understand the real tradeoffs?
- **User empathy for novel experiences** — AI products create new UX patterns. You need instinct for what works when there's no playbook.
- **Technical credibility** — Strong comfort with APIs, model behavior, prompt engineering, fine-tuning concepts
- **Velocity** — OpenAI ships fast. You need to operate well under speed + uncertainty.
- **Systems thinking** — Understand second-order effects of AI product decisions (misuse, hallucination, over-reliance)
- **Business acumen** — OpenAI is balancing mission and commercial survival. PMs must understand how API + consumer + enterprise revenue fit together.

---

## Interview Process (PM Roles)

1. **Recruiter screen** (~30 min)
2. **Hiring manager interview** — Product thinking + background
3. **Take-home or case** — Often a written product exercise
4. **On-site loop** (varies by team):
   - Product design / product sense
   - Strategy round
   - Analytical round
   - Behavioral / cross-functional
   - Technical / AI knowledge round (for some roles)

## Confirmed / Representative Interview Questions

### AI / Technical
- How would you handle hallucinations in a generative AI model deployed to users?
- What are the ethical risks of deploying agentic AI in high-stakes environments?
- How would you adapt a generative AI model to perform well in a specific domain?
- Define hallucinations in LLMs.
- What's your approach to prompt engineering?
- How do you approach GenAI safety in consumer products?

### Product / Strategy
- How would you design ChatGPT's monetization for the next 3 years?
- What's the biggest risk to OpenAI's market position?
- Should OpenAI build a consumer hardware device?
- How would you improve ChatGPT for enterprise customers?
- How would you measure success for ChatGPT's voice mode?
- Design a new product or feature for OpenAI's API platform.
- How would you grow OpenAI's API usage across customer segments?

### Analytical
- What metrics would you use to track the growth of OpenAI's API usage?
- How would you measure user satisfaction with ChatGPT responses?
- ChatGPT's daily active sessions dropped 10% week-over-week. What do you investigate?

### Behavioral
- Why OpenAI?
- Tell me about a time you made a product decision under significant uncertainty.
- Tell me about a time you had to balance speed and safety/quality.
- Tell me about a time you influenced a team without direct authority.

## Tips Specific to OpenAI

- **Use ChatGPT and Claude extensively** — Know both products from the inside. Be ready to compare them honestly.
- **Have a point of view on AI safety** — Not a rehearsed one. A genuine one. Where do you think the real risks are?
- **Think about novel UX patterns** — AI products don't follow traditional UX conventions. Show you've thought about this.
- **Know the API ecosystem** — OpenAI's API is a massive business. Understand developer use cases and pain points.
- **Competitors:** Anthropic (Claude), Google (Gemini), Meta (Llama), Mistral, Cohere
- **Be comfortable with uncertainty** — OpenAI's product roadmap is shaped by research breakthroughs that can't be fully predicted. Show you thrive in this environment.
