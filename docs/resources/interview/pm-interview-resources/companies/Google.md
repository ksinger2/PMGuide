# Google — PM Interview Profile

## Company Overview
- **Mission:** Organize the world's information and make it universally accessible and useful
- **Products:** Search, YouTube, Android, Chrome, Google Cloud, Google Maps, Gmail, Google Workspace, Pixel, Gemini (AI), Waymo
- **Culture:** Data-driven, user-first, structured thinking. "Googliness" — intellectual humility, collaborative, curious.
- **Size:** ~180,000 employees
- **Business model:** Advertising (~75% of revenue), Cloud (fast-growing), Hardware

---

## What Google Looks For in PMs

- **Structured thinking at scale** — Products serve billions of users. Every decision has massive downstream effects.
- **User-first mindset** — Features must solve real user problems, not just hit business KPIs
- **Product craft** — Google values PMs who obsess over the details of the user experience
- **Technical credibility** — You don't need to code, but you must understand technical tradeoffs and speak credibly with engineers
- **Data rigor** — Strong comfort with metrics, A/B testing, defining success precisely
- **Clear written + verbal communication** — Google interviews are heavily craft-focused. "When do you consider a design review complete?" is a real Google question.
- **Intellectual curiosity** — Show genuine interest in the problem space, not just the answer

---

## Interview Process (PM Roles)

1. **Resume screen**
2. **Recruiter screen** (~30 min) — Background, interest in Google, logistics
3. **Phone interview** (~45 min) — Product design or strategy question + behavioral
4. **On-site loop** (4-5 rounds):
   - **Product design** — Design/improve a product
   - **Product strategy** — Market entry, competitive, or growth strategy
   - **Analytical** — Metrics, A/B testing, success definition
   - **Craft/Execution** — "When do you consider X complete?" style questions
   - **Behavioral/Leadership** — STAR stories, cross-functional collaboration
5. **Hiring committee review** — Scores aggregated; committee makes final call

**Note:** Google has phased out technical PM questions for most PM roles.

---

## Google's Key Interview Style

Google interviews are **craft-focused and structured**. They want to see:
- Clear problem decomposition
- Rigorous success metric definition
- User empathy grounded in data
- Intellectual honesty about tradeoffs

**"Craft" questions** are Google's signature — e.g.:
- "When do you consider a design review complete?"
- "How do you know when to ship vs. keep iterating?"
- "How do you define done?"

These test PM philosophy and operational maturity, not just product thinking.

## Confirmed Interview Questions (Real, Sourced)

### Product Design / Sense
- Design a vending machine for a hotel
- Design a product for airport travelers
- Design a product to improve the quality of life of elderly people
- Design a product for riders of a bus company that is retrofitting existing buses to make them driverless. *(Assume the driving part is taken care of)*
- You are walking on a sidewalk and find a device that accurately predicts the weather. Build a product for this device.
- Imagine self-driving cars are a thing (Waymo). For the console in a middle of a car, pick a Google product and design it for first time product. What should Google build or extend? What features would you add or adjust? Can't be Google Maps.
- Design Netflix for kids
- You are lead PM for Google Photos that has 1B downloads. Introduce a paid tier for users *(assume it was not present till now)*
- Choose an app you use on a regular basis. What do you like about it and how would you improve it?
- Imagine you are the PM for Google Photos. Design the Year-in-review feature for it.
- As a PM for sidewalk labs, improve Parking meter for users
- Pick a product — what do you like about it, what can be improved? What would be your vision for where you would take this product next? For adding a feature, how would you execute on that as a PM? How do you approach different perspectives from different functions? How do you resolve issues? How do you align the team on the vision for launching new features?

### Product Strategy / Vision
- Strat Q: TPUs are historically a moat for GCP *(internal only)*. But big clients like Apple/Anthropic want on-prem hardware to avoid cloud lock-in. As TPU Lead: Do we decouple and sell chips directly? If yes: Pitch a CIO currently standardized on Nvidia H100s. If no: Explain the strategic risk vs Nvidia's monopoly.
- You are the PM for Apple Maps. Your goal is to win Market Share. What do you do? What about marketing, if it already has all the features, how can you market it?
- What technology/product/service do you think will be obsolete in 5 years and why? Now imagine you're the PM at said company, how would you advise their strategy?
- Pick any product that you think will become obsolete in the next 3-4 years. Imagine you are the CPO of that product. What will you do to avoid becoming obsolete?
- Should Google expand to emerging countries for home services or double down on existing countries?
- You're the head of product at YouTube. How would you think about leveraging genAI?
- What would you build to make airport travel easier? 1. YouTube shorts view time is up, other video content is down — what do you do?
- How is AI changing the landscape of software engineering, change the landscape moving forward. What process would you take to decide where to focus to make the biggest impact on developer productivity?
- Tell me about a product you like, that is not a tech product ideally, and tell me about why you like it. *(Like a running shoe or tennis racket, backpack, etc.)* If you were the PM, if you are the product owner, and you are setting out to design a roadmap for V2, how would you go about doing it?
- Imagine we now live in a post autonomous vehicle world. You are the CEO of FedEx and you missed the boat. What are your actions? *(CEO level — Vision, System Design, Product Portfolio Strategy, Capital Allocation)*
- What would you do going about what feature to prioritize for either Google Maps or YouTube? What specific feature would you implement? *(Listed general buckets for first part: analyzing user segments, market competitors, etc.; second part was standard product design approach)*
- How would you increase Pixel Buds by 50%?
- 1) What's the product you hate the most? How would you improve it? 2) What would be your product strategy for this product you've applied to be a PM for?

### Analytical / Metrics
- What metrics would you track to evaluate a new feature?
- How would you measure success if Spotify launches Podcasts?
- Imagine you are the PM for pricing. How would you price YouTube Premium? *(Clarify: Region/Which YouTube Product, Goal, User Segmentation, Experiment, Decision, Summarize)*
- 1. You are a PM at Google. Create a product for airport travelers. 2. Behavioral questions — 15 minutes
- You are PM for Google Reels. How would you define success?

### Technical
- Design a scalable data warehouse architecture to support analytics and reporting needs. *(System design)*
- If your generative model needs to handle very long inputs beyond typical context limits, how would you manage or compress that information?
- Pick a computer science topic of your choice and teach it to a middle schooler thinking about studying computer science.

### Craft / Execution (Google-Specific)
- How do you work out the scheduling and timelines with the engineering team?
- How do you approach different perspectives from different functions (design and engineering)? How do you resolve issues?
- How do you align the team on the vision for launching new features?

### Behavioral / "Googliness"
- In your team, someone things he or she is not a good fit? How will you handle the situation.
- Your CEO gives you X million USD and asked you build a competitive product. How will you handle?
- Tell me about a time you had to convince someone.
- Tell me about a time you pitched an innovation.
- Tell me about a time you worked with a difficult stakeholder.
- Your goal is to save 20% time for sellers who sell Ads to Big enterprises. How would you go about it? What data would you need? Now imagine, you've developed a product — AI chatbot that creates dynamic PowerPoint presentations. How would you go about measuring success?
- Imagine you're in an exit interview with current leadership. What advice would you give them? What's stopping them from doing that today?
- Assume you're in charge of organizing a team building event at work. You decided to go with the most popular option among the team but some people said they won't attend. How will you handle this?
- What's your favorite product? How would you improve it?
- Google invents teleportation. How would you price it?
- Favorite product *(strategic insights format)*

## Tips Specific to Google

- **Think at scale** — Google operates at billions of users. Mention scale implications in your answers.
- **Define success precisely** — Google loves rigorous metric definition. Don't be vague.
- **Show user empathy grounded in data** — Not just "users want X" but "users struggle with X based on Y signal"
- **Know the AI landscape** — Google is in an AI arms race. Gemini vs. GPT-4/Claude is highly relevant context.
- **Understand the ads business** — Even non-ads products affect Google's ad revenue indirectly
- **Be prepared for craft questions** — Google uniquely tests PM philosophy and operational maturity
- **Competitors to know:** OpenAI (Gemini vs. GPT), Microsoft (Bing AI, Azure), Amazon (AWS, Alexa), Meta, Apple
