# Roblox — PM Interview Profile

## Company Overview
- **Mission:** Connect a billion people with optimism and civility
- **Known for:** UGC gaming platform, creator economy, Robux virtual currency, young demographic (majority under 17)
- **Culture:** Creator-first, community-driven, mission-oriented around safe online connection for younger users
- **Size:** ~4,000 employees
- **Business model:** Robux (virtual currency) — users buy Robux, spend in games; creators earn Robux (converted to real money via DevEx). Advertising layer launching.

---

## What Roblox Looks For in PMs

- **Platform thinking** — Roblox is a platform, not a single product. Understanding the creator-user-platform flywheel is essential.
- **Creator empathy** — Deep understanding of creator needs: tools, monetization, distribution, community
- **Safety-first mindset** — With a majority-minor user base, every product decision has child safety implications
- **Balance of stakeholders** — You're constantly balancing creators, users (many of them kids), parents, advertisers, and the platform
- **Community + social dynamics** — Understand how communities form, grow, and sometimes break on platforms
- **Monetization sophistication** — Robux economy is complex; virtual goods, game passes, subscriptions, and the DevEx program
- **Growth thinking** — Roblox is expanding demographics (older users, international markets) — understand that strategy

---

## Interview Process (PM Roles)

1. **Recruiter screen**
2. **Hiring manager interview**
3. **On-site loop** (typically 4-5 rounds):
   - Product design / product sense
   - Product strategy
   - Execution / analytics
   - Behavioral / leadership
   - Cross-functional (eng, design, or data)

---

## Roblox Platform Flywheel

```
Creators build experiences
    ↓
Users play and spend Robux
    ↓
Creators earn Robux (DevEx to $)
    ↓
Creators invest in better experiences
    ↓
More users attracted → cycle continues
```

**Key tension:** Creator monetization vs. user safety (especially minors spending money)

## Confirmed / Representative Interview Questions

### Product Design
- How would you improve Roblox's Creator Hub?
- Design parental controls for Roblox that don't kill engagement.
- How would you design a new monetization feature for Roblox creators?
- How would you improve Roblox's onboarding experience for new users?
- Design a social feature for Roblox that helps players find friends.
- How would you expand Roblox to attract users over 17?

### Strategy
- How would you grow Roblox's creator base by 2x?
- Should Roblox expand into the education market?
- How would you approach Roblox's international expansion in India?
- What is the biggest threat to Roblox over the next 5 years?
- How would you increase Robux spending among existing users?
- Should Roblox build a standalone mobile app separate from the main platform?

### Execution / Analytical
- Creator retention on Roblox dropped 15%. What do you investigate?
- Robux spending is up but game session length is down. What does that mean and what do you do?
- How would you measure success for Roblox's Creator Hub?
- What are the key metrics for the Roblox creator economy?
- How would you A/B test a new creator monetization feature?

### Behavioral
- Tell me about a time you balanced the needs of multiple stakeholders with conflicting interests.
- Tell me about a time you made a product decision with child safety implications.
- Tell me about a time you built something for a community that didn't respond as expected.
- Why Roblox?

## Tips Specific to Roblox

- **Play Roblox** — Seriously. Experience it as a user and explore a few popular games. Know what creators build.
- **Understand the creator economy deeply** — Robux, DevEx rates, creator pain points, top creator strategies
- **Child safety is non-negotiable** — Every product answer should acknowledge safety implications for minors without being asked
- **Think platform, not product** — Your job is to enable creators to build great things, not to build everything yourself
- **Know the demographic tension** — Roblox wants to grow its 17+ user base without losing its core younger demographic
- **Competitors:** Minecraft (Microsoft), Fortnite (Epic Games), Discord (community layer), YouTube Gaming
