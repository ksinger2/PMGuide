# PM Interview Rubrics

Rating scale: **Very Weak → Weak → Neutral → Strong → Very Strong**

---

## Product Design Rubric

| Criteria | Signal | Very Weak | Weak | Neutral | Strong | Very Strong |
|----------|--------|-----------|------|---------|--------|-------------|
| **Product Design Skill** | Business acumen | No understanding of business context | Struggled to tie to business goals | Unclear/flawed business discussion | Clearly discussed goals, positioning, trends | Nuanced landscape understanding, insightful arguments |
| | User-centricity | Failed to consider end user | Struggled to anchor on users | Attempted user-centric design, missed key points | Discussed pain points & opportunities, prioritized well | Analyzed users accurately, prioritized effectively, recalled users throughout |
| | Product vision | Failed to discuss future of product | Struggled to articulate vision | Laid out possible future with minor errors | Showed thoughtfulness and intuition on product vision | Exemplary product intuition; data-backed arguments tied to UX |
| **Critical Thinking** | Clarifying questions | Failed to ask questions/interact | Struggled to ask right questions, made assumptions | Asked good clarifying questions, missed key points | Asked insightful questions, adapted design to fit | Asked surprising, insightful questions; came up with high-quality novel designs |
| | Tradeoffs & errors | Failed to mention tradeoffs | Mentioned but didn't justify tradeoffs | Covered errors and tradeoffs, could have made better choices | Logical tradeoff discussion; correctly identified possible errors | Deep knowledge of tradeoffs; offered alternatives; neatly summarized pros/cons |
| | Passion & creativity | Failed to show enthusiasm or creative thinking | Gave bland solutions / incorrect judgment calls | Displayed interest and reasonable insight, nothing exceptional | Showed extensive knowledge, enthusiasm, and creativity | Gave inspired answers; showed clear passion |
| **Cultural Fit** | Communication | Failed to communicate clearly despite prompts | Poor communication; interviewer had trouble following | Communication varied — clear in some areas, vague in others | Good skills; articulated thought process clearly and consistently | Clear communication; anticipated questions; justified decisions; checked in often |
| | Collaboration | Failed to take lead, didn't respond to guidance | Struggled to stay on track without guidance | Took lead and performed well, but needed redirects | Effectively led discussion; involved the interviewer | Took lead and made exceptional use of interviewer; discussion was collaborative |

## Product Strategy Rubric

| Criteria | Signal | Very Weak | Weak | Neutral | Strong | Very Strong |
|----------|--------|-----------|------|---------|--------|-------------|
| **Product Strategy Skill** | Competitive analysis | Failed to consider competition | Poor understanding of competitive landscape | Fair understanding of key competitors and trends | Extensive insight into relevant competitors and positioning | Deeply aware of landscape; discussed competitive strategies logically and creatively |
| | Market analysis | Failed to consider the market | Poor understanding of market | Fair understanding of key market forces | Significant knowledge of relevant market dynamics | Deep market insight; discussed opportunities logically and creatively |
| | Business strategy | Failed to consider business goals | Strategy unclear or full of errors | Basic business sense; fair strategy with some guidance | Delivered logical strategy that fit greater business context | Strategic and nuanced understanding; compelling arguments |
| | Roadmapping | Failed to articulate a product roadmap | Roadmap unclear or full of errors | Built fair product roadmap with some guidance | Roadmap fits goals and landscape; logical assumptions and alternatives | Roadmap clear, compelling, and adaptive to change |
| **Critical Thinking** | Clarifying questions | Failed to ask questions/interact | Struggled to ask good questions / made faulty assumptions | Asked good questions but missed key points | Asked insightful questions and adapted strategy to fit | Scoped problem well; gave high-quality creative strategy |
| | Tradeoffs/errors | Failed to mention tradeoffs or possible errors | Mentioned but didn't justify tradeoffs; made faulty judgment | Covered errors and tradeoffs; could have made better choices | Discussed tradeoffs logically and identified possible errors | Deep knowledge of tradeoffs; alternatives and pros/cons offered |
| | Passion/creativity | Failed to show enthusiasm or creative thinking | Solutions bland / didn't show interest | Some interest and reasonable insight, nothing exceptional | Showed impressive knowledge, enthusiasm, and creativity | Gave inspired answers; showed clear passion |
| **Cultural Fit** | Communication | Failed to communicate clearly despite prompts | Poor communication; trouble following despite prompts | Varied — clear in some areas, vague in others | Good communication; explained logic clearly and consistently | Clear communication; anticipated questions; justified decision; checked in often |
| | Collaboration | Failed to take lead; didn't respond to guidance | Struggled to stay on track without guidance | Led/performed well, but needed redirection/hints | Effectively led discussion; involved the interviewer | Took lead and made exceptional use of interviewer; discussion was collaborative |

## Technical Rubric

| Criteria | Signal | Very Weak | Weak | Neutral | Strong | Very Strong |
|----------|--------|-----------|------|---------|--------|-------------|
| **Analytical Skill** | Data literacy | Failed to use data to answer | Failed to identify obvious patterns in data | Showed reasonable ability to reason with data, missed key points | Used data effectively; asked good questions; made solid assumptions | Extracted valuable data insights; identified key patterns; suggested reports/tests |
| | Comfort with metrics | Failed to show basic understanding of relevant metrics | Struggled to define metrics / compare different metrics | Showed reasonable knowledge, missed "best fit" metrics | Clearly discussed pros and cons of various metrics; solid argument for choices | Thoroughly and accurately discussed metrics; recommended ways to streamline analytical processes |
| **Critical Thinking** | Clarifying questions | Failed to ask questions / interact | Struggled to ask right questions / made assumptions without clarifying | Asked good questions but missed key points | Asked insightful questions and adapted strategy to fit | Scoped problem effectively; delivered high-quality, creative strategy |
| | Tradeoffs & errors | Failed to mention tradeoffs and possible errors | Mentioned tradeoffs but failed to justify / made incorrect judgment | Covered possible errors and tradeoffs, could have made better choices | Discussed tradeoffs logically and correctly identified possible errors | Deep knowledge of tradeoffs; offered alternatives; neatly summarized pros/cons |
| **Cultural Fit** | Software implementation | Failed to show basic knowledge of how software is implemented | Unable to dive deeply into implementation process | Understands basic software implementation | Solid understanding of technical processes underlying software products | Deep, engineering-level understanding of software |
| | Technical communication | Failed to communicate with technical team members | Struggled to translate "PM-speak" into technical terms; needed significant clarification | Effective at translating requirements into technical terms | Communicated requirements/requests with technical accuracy; could partake in "live" technical discussion | Matches technical team in ability to communicate technically |

## Execution Rubric

| Criteria | Signal | Very Weak | Weak | Neutral | Strong | Very Strong |
|----------|--------|-----------|------|---------|--------|-------------|
| **Execution Skill** | Variable isolation | Failed to isolate variables when problem-solving | Variable isolation was sloppy or had obvious errors | Isolated variables with reasonable skill, with a nudge or two | Isolated variables effectively; accounted for likelihood, urgency, cost/benefit, testability | Isolated variables admirably; resulting action plan was elegant and robust |
| | Data literacy | Failed to use data to answer the question | Failed to identify obvious patterns in data | Reasonable ability to reason with data, but missed key points | Problem was effectively broken down and worked through in a logical way | Extracted valuable data insights; identified key patterns; suggested reports/tests; made logical arguments |
| **Critical Thinking** | Logical thinking | Failed to probe the origin of the problem; rushed into an answer | Displayed some logical thought but made serious errors or needed significant guidance | Thought processes were somewhat logical despite a few errors | Strong sense of likely problem sources; able to test and confirm this intuition effectively | Flawless logic; no mistakes or oversights; clearly articulated logical thinking |
| | Problem solving | Failed to consider context; showed no intuition for where problems likely originate | Intuition was incorrect and/or not justifiable when pressed | Reasonable knowledge and intuition for where problems are likely to originate | Good communication skills; articulated thought process clearly and consistently | Deep intuition for how the "system" works; plan and solution reflected this |
| **Cultural Fit** | Communication | Failed to communicate clearly despite repeated prompts | Poor communication throughout; interviewer had trouble following | Varied — clear in some areas, vague/incomplete in others | Effectively led the discussion; involved the interviewer throughout | Clear communication; anticipated questions; articulated reasons for decision; checked in throughout |
| | Collaboration | Failed to take the lead; didn't respond to guidance | Struggled to stay on track without guidance | Took the lead and performed well, but needed redirects or hints | Communicated requirements/requests with technical accuracy; could partake in "live" technical discussion | Took the lead and made exceptional use of interviewer; discussion was more collaboration than interview |

## Analytical Rubric

| Criteria | Signal | Very Weak | Weak | Neutral | Strong | Very Strong |
|----------|--------|-----------|------|---------|--------|-------------|
| **Data Literacy** | Data literacy | Failed to use data to answer | Failed to identify obvious patterns in data | Reasonable ability to reason with data, missed key points | Used data effectively; good questions, solid assumptions, logical conclusions | Valuable data insights and logical arguments; identified key patterns and reports to run |
| | Comfort with metrics | Failed to show basic understanding of relevant metrics | Struggled to define metrics / compare different metrics | Reasonable knowledge, missed "best fit" metrics | Clearly discussed pros and cons; made a solid argument for choices | Thoroughly discussed metrics; proposed ways to streamline analytical processes |
| **Testing** | Diagnosis | Failed to probe the origin of the problem; rushed into an answer | Attempted to diagnose but made serious errors or needed significant guidance | Fair diagnosis, but missed nuances | Asked good questions and used answers effectively to scope a solvable problem | Asked insightful questions; set up a clear, easy-to-use test plan |
| | Prioritization | Failed to prioritize efforts when solving a problem | Prioritization was sloppy or had obvious errors | Fair prioritization after a nudge or two | Prioritized effectively; accounted for urgency, cost/benefit, testability | Prioritized admirably; proposed plan provided maximum benefits with minimal cost |
| | Execution | Failed to define a clear plan to solve the problem | Struggled to communicate action plan in an executable way | Well-defined plan but missed key points, or good intuition but plan wasn't fully executable | Correctly identified key elements of the problem; provided KPIs and logical solutions | Comprehensive plan; best-fit KPIs, bottlenecks addressed, other solutions given |
| **Cultural Fit** | Collaboration | Failed to take the lead; didn't respond to guidance | Struggled to stay on track without guidance | Took the lead and performed well, but may have needed redirects | Effectively led the discussion and involved the interviewer | Took the lead and made exceptional use of interviewer; discussion was collaborative |
| | Curiosity | Failed to show interest and/or ask good questions | Attempted to collect information but failed to intuit right areas to dig into | Asked good questions and/or had good ideas, but missed key points | Asked great questions and worked to understand the entire problem context | Gathered information effectively; asked insightful questions that got to root of issue |

## Estimation Rubric

|----------|--------|-----------|------|---------|--------|-------------|
| **Critical Thinking** | Problem solving | Failed to ask questions; rushed into an answer | Serious errors or significant guidance needed in scoping | Scoped the problem, but missed key elements | Asked good questions and used answers effectively to scope a solvable problem | Asked insightful questions; set up an easy calculation and an accurate answer |
| | Comfort with numbers | Failed to calculate on the fly or show competence with numerical relationships | Attempts to quantify were sloppy or needed lots of guidance | Arrived at a fair answer with a nudge or two | Made accurate and quick calculations; gave helpful and appropriate approximations | Above-average ease in using numbers to an advantage; creative use of approximation, "rules of thumb" |
| | Possible errors | Failed to discuss possible errors | Failed to reasonably justify decisions / made bad judgment calls | Correctly identified some possible mistakes but missed others | Correctly identified possible errors and suggested ways to increase accuracy | Covered possible errors thoroughly; offered alternatives; neatly summarized pros and cons |
| **Cultural Fit** | Communication | Failed to communicate clearly despite repeated prompts | Poor communication; interviewer had trouble following | Varied — clear in some areas, vague/incomplete in others | Good skills; articulated thought process clearly and consistently | Clear communication; anticipated questions; articulated reasons for decision; checked in throughout |
| | Collaboration | Failed to take the lead; didn't respond to guidance | Struggled to stay on track without guidance | Took the lead and performed well, but needed redirects or hints | Effectively led the discussion and involved the interviewer | Took the lead and made exceptional use of interviewer; discussion was more collaboration than interview |
| | Creativity | Failed to show enthusiasm or creative thinking | Solutions were bland; didn't show interest in the problem | Showed reasonable insight, but nothing exceptional | Demonstrated logical and creative thought processes | Displayed exceptional creativity and outside-the-box thinking; maintained logic and found novel solutions |
