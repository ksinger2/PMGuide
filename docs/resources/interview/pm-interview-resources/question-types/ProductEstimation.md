# Estimation Questions

## What This Tests
Structured decomposition, comfort with numbers, problem scoping, ability to reason under uncertainty.

**Key insight:** Interviewers don't expect the exact answer. They care about HOW you solve the problem.

---

## 5-Step Framework

1. **Scope the problem** — Ask clarifying questions. State assumptions upfront. Prevent going in the wrong direction.
2. **Break it down** — Find a high-level equation that describes the problem. Break each factor into estimable pieces. Make simplifying assumptions as needed.
3. **Estimate unknowns** — Use strategies below. Gut-check numbers as you go.
4. **Answer** — Plug estimates into your equation. Convert units. Round for simplicity. Communicate each step.
5. **Explain why you're wrong** — Summarize assumptions that felt like guesses. Describe what you'd reconsider with more time. Shows critical thinking.

---

## Strategies for Estimating Unknowns

- **Estimate via proxy** — Substitute unknown for something familiar (e.g., to estimate cars in Seattle, relate to households × car ownership rate)
- **Segment large groups** — Break diverse groups into smaller segments with distinct behaviors, then sum
- **Use personal references** — Relate to something you know personally, then account for bias
- **Define upper and lower bounds** — What's the realistic min and max? Use average as starting point

## Avoiding Math Errors
- Round difficult numbers (334M → 300M)
- Split multiplications by separating powers of ten
- Move decimal places for powers of ten

## Increasing Accuracy
- Gut-check: if a number seems too high or low, revisit assumptions
- Put answer in context: compare to related metrics you trust
- Check against competitors: use benchmarks when possible

## Question Bank

### Market Size
- What will the market size be for driverless cars in 2025?
- Estimate the market size for AI coding assistants in the US.
- How big is the podcasting ad market?

### Product/Revenue Estimation
- How much does the Google Play Store make in a year?
- Estimate Airbnb's annual revenue.
- Estimate how many videos are watched on YouTube per day.
- How much money does the App Store make annually?

### Supply/Demand
- Estimate the number of Uber drivers needed in the San Francisco Bay Area.
- How many bikes would you need to start a bikeshare company in New York City?
- Estimate the number of restaurants in San Francisco.

### Physical / Unusual
- How much does the Empire State Building weigh?
- Estimate internet bandwidth needed for a college campus.
- Estimate how many shampoo bottles are sold in the US per year.

### Self-Driving / Future Tech
- Estimate the number of self-driving cars on the road in 2030.

Key signals:
- **Problem solving** — scope before calculating; ask clarifying questions
- **Comfort with numbers** — fast, clean math; smart approximations
- **Possible errors** — proactively surface your assumptions and where you could be wrong
- **Communication** — narrate every step; make thinking visible
- **Creativity** — find clever proxies and novel approaches
- **Collaboration** — check in with the interviewer; make it a dialogue
