# Behavioral Questions

## What This Tests
PM philosophy, leadership, conflict resolution, culture fit, how you handle real situations.

---

## STAR Framework

- **S**ituation — Set the context. Brief.
- **T**ask — What did you need to accomplish?
- **A**ction — What did *you specifically* do? Use "I" not "we."
- **R**esult — Quantified outcome if possible. What did you learn?

**Each story should be under 2 minutes.**

---

## Story Bank Strategy

Build 6-8 diverse stories before interviews. Map each to common themes:

| Theme | What to Prepare |
|-------|----------------|
| Leadership / influence without authority | Led cross-functional work, got buy-in |
| Conflict resolution | Disagreement with eng, design, leadership |
| Bold/difficult decision | Made a call with limited data or high stakes |
| Failure / learning | Something went wrong; what you learned |
| Ambiguity | Navigated unclear situation without guidance |
| Customer focus | Changed direction based on user insight |
| Data-driven decision | Used data to make or change a call |
| Negative feedback | Gave or received critical feedback |
| Prioritization | Said no to something important |

**Tips:**
- Be specific — concrete details are more memorable than generalities
- Always end with results — quantify where possible
- Own your contribution — "I" not "we"
- Tailor to company values — research what the company cares about

## Favorite Product (3 Ps)

- **Passion** — Choose a product you genuinely use and know deeply
- **Perspective** — Have a nuanced, defensible opinion
- **Personality** — Your choice should reveal your PM values

**Structure:**
1. What is it and what does it do?
2. Who is it for?
3. What problem does it solve?
4. Why is it your favorite? (your unique angle)
5. How would you improve it?

**Tips:**
- Avoid choosing the interviewer's own company product (can seem sycophantic)
- Show analytical thinking, not just enthusiasm
- Know its competitors and what makes it better

---

## Why This Company

What they're evaluating: genuine interest, research done, values alignment, how your skills match their needs.

**Structure:**
1. What specifically about the company/mission excites you
2. How your background maps to their current challenges
3. What you hope to contribute

## Question Bank

### Conflict
- Tell me about a time you had a conflict with an engineering counterpart.
- Tell me about a time you had a conflict with someone. How did you resolve it and what did you learn?
- Tell me about a time you faced a conflict while on a team.
- Tell me about a time you had to deliver negative feedback.
- Tell me about a time you received negative feedback and how you handled it.

### Leadership / Influence
- Tell me about a time you had to get leadership to buy into your vision.
- Tell me about a time you made a bold and difficult decision.
- Tell me about a time you had to make a short-term sacrifice for a long-term gain.

### Ambiguity / Problem Solving
- How do you deal with ambiguous situations?
- Tell me about a time you solved a customer pain point.
- Tell me about a time you fixed a cross-functional issue.

### Self-Awareness
- What's your biggest strength?
- What's your biggest weakness?
- How do you prioritize tasks?

### Company Fit
- Why do you want to work at [Company]?
- What is your favorite product and why?
- How would you launch a new product to our existing user base?

### AI-Specific (Anthropic / OpenAI)
- How do you approach GenAI safety in consumer products?
- What Gen AI skills do you think will be critical in the next year?
- What's your approach to prompt engineering?

---

## Rubric Signals to Hit

Behavioral questions are primarily evaluated on:
- **Communication** — clear, concise, structured story
- **Self-awareness** — honest about what you did and what you learned
- **Impact** — results are quantified and meaningful
- **Culture fit** — values align with company; you'd thrive in their environment
- **Specificity** — concrete details, not vague generalities
