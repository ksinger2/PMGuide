# Execution Questions

## What This Tests
Root cause analysis, decision-making, variable isolation, data-driven problem solving, logical thinking.

---

## Two Main Types

### Type 1: Root Cause Analysis (RCA)
A metric changed unexpectedly. Figure out why and what to do.

**RCA Framework (6 Steps):**
1. **Clarify** — Scope, timeline, context. Platform? User segment? Geography? When did it start?
2. **Explore internal factors** — Recent product/feature changes? Backend or infra changes? A/B tests running?
3. **Explore external factors** — Competitors affected? Seasonal effects? News events? Macroeconomic?
4. **Form & test hypotheses** — Generate hypotheses. Ask data questions to validate or eliminate each one. Narrow systematically.
5. **Identify root cause** — State most likely root cause with evidence. Communicate every step.
6. **Propose solutions** — Immediate fix + longer-term improvements

**Key:** Ask concrete, specific questions. Iterate hypotheses as data comes in. Interviewer plays data analyst — use them.

---

### Type 2: Decision-Making
A product decision with tradeoffs. Often no obvious winner.

**Decision-Making Framework (6 Steps):**
1. **Clarify** — Understand context, confirm you're addressing the right question
2. **Define product & company goals** — What does success look like? What is being optimized for?
3. **Define decision criteria** — Based on goals, establish criteria. Weight them by importance.
4. **Evaluate options against criteria** — Systematically compare. Be explicit about tradeoffs.
5. **Make decision & defend it** — State recommendation clearly. Explain reasoning, acknowledge risks.
6. **Discuss next steps** — What would you monitor? What would change your decision?

**Common pitfall:** Failing to define criteria before evaluating options → wishy-washy answers.

## Question Bank

### RCA Questions
- Facebook friend requests are down 10%. What do you do?
- You're a PM at Lyft and there's been a 20% increase in ride cancellations. What would you do?
- TikTok daily usage is declining. What do you investigate?
- Nike's conversion rate dropped. Walk me through your diagnosis.
- Flipkart cart additions are down. What would you do?
- You're a PM at Stripe and a merchant is noticing an increase in fraud. How would you solve this?
- Instagram Stories views dropped 15% week-over-week. What do you do?

### Decision-Making Questions
- You're a PM on Facebook Watch. A redesign improved watch time but dropped likes and comments. Do you ship it?
- You're a PM at Instagram. How would you decide between showing Stories for 24 hours vs. 48 hours?
- Reactions are up 20% but comments are down 10%. What would you do?
- Determine the right number of photos to show in Facebook's Newsfeed.

### Goal-Setting / Roadmapping
- Set goals for Google Maps.
- Set goals for Facebook Marketplace.
- Set goals for Facebook Reactions.
- What goal would you set for a new AI assistant product?

## Rubric Signals to Hit
See `rubrics/Rubric.md` → Execution Rubric

Key signals:
- **Variable isolation** — don't conflate causes; isolate systematically
- **Data literacy** — use data to drive every hypothesis and conclusion
- **Logical thinking** — probe origin before jumping to answers
- **Problem solving** — show intuition for how the system works
- **Communication** — walk the interviewer through your thinking at every step
- **Collaboration** — use the interviewer as a data source; make it a dialogue
