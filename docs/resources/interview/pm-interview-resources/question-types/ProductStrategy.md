# Product Strategy Questions

## What This Tests
Market analysis, competitive analysis, business strategy, roadmapping, long-term product thinking.

---

## 7-Step Framework

1. **Clarify** — Scope nouns, verbs, assumptions. Eliminate vagueness. Understand ecosystem context (how does this fit with existing products and strategy?).
2. **Define company/product goals** — Key value drivers, existing strategies, strengths/weaknesses. How does this connect to the broader product portfolio?
3. **Define the landscape** — Market & competition, public opinion, technology (existing + emerging), regulation. Segment the market: who are the 2-3 key user/customer segments? How are they affected differently by competitive and market forces?
4. **Define guiding principles** — Snapshot of situation capturing business rationale and priority (e.g. "Customer trust is our #1 priority"). Which segments does each principle prioritize?
5. **Establish option set** — Generate ideas, then filter weak options based on principles. Compare options by segment impact — which segments benefit, which are underserved?
6. **Make decision & argue for it** — Evaluate by: Scale, Impact (by segment), Cost, Risk, Likelihood of success, Additional benefits, Ecosystem effects. Justify segment prioritization with TAM/growth/strategic fit.
7. **Evaluate & recap** — Cross-segment tradeoffs (what are we sacrificing?), ecosystem implications, what you'd do differently with more time, how you'd mitigate risks

## Sub-Types

### Go-to-Market (GTM) Strategy
Key elements: Target customers, Value proposition, Pricing, Distribution channels, Marketing approach, Success metrics

Framework:
1. Define target market and user segments
2. Articulate value proposition for each segment
3. Determine pricing model
4. Identify distribution channels
5. Define marketing/acquisition approach
6. Set success metrics

### Pricing Strategy
Key models: Cost-plus, Value-based, Competitive, Freemium, Subscription, Usage-based

Framework:
1. Understand who is buying and why
2. Understand the value delivered
3. Understand competitive pricing
4. Evaluate different pricing models
5. Make a recommendation and defend it

### Growth Strategy (AARRR Framework)
- **A**cquisition — how users find you
- **A**ctivation — first great experience
- **R**etention — users come back
- **R**evenue — monetization
- **R**eferral — users tell others

Framework:
1. Understand current state — baseline metrics, trends
2. Identify growth levers across AARRR
3. Prioritize by impact and effort
4. Propose experiments/tests
5. Define success metrics

## Common Pitfalls
- Jumping straight to a solution without setting context
- Not answering the specific question asked
- Failing to consider tradeoffs
- Weak competitive analysis
- No clear recommendation — don't be wishy-washy
- **Treating the market as monolithic** — always segment customers/users and show how strategy affects each
- **Ignoring ecosystem effects** — how does this strategy impact other products, teams, or user segments?
- **Hiding segment trade-offs** — every strategic choice prioritizes some segments over others; name the cost

## Question Bank

### Market Entry
- Should Amazon enter the food delivery business?
- Should Google enter the healthcare market?
- Should Google enter ridesharing?
- Samsung gaming market entry — should they do it?

### Threat / Competitive Analysis
- What is the biggest threat to YouTube over the next 5 years?
- What is the biggest risk to OpenAI's market position?
- What is the biggest threat to Netflix?

### Growth
- How would you increase Airbnb bookings by 20%?
- How would you 10x Netflix revenue?
- How would you grow Anthropic's revenue without compromising the safety mission?

### Pricing
- How would you price a new line of high-end mineral water bottles for the US market?
- How would you price Google Maps for business customers?

### GTM
- How would you launch Google Maps in a new country?
- How would you launch a new product to an existing user base?

### Ambiguous/Open
- What should Airbnb do about short-term rentals being banned in major cities?
- How do you approach GenAI safety in consumer products? (Anthropic)
- What Gen AI skills will be critical in the next year? (Anthropic)
